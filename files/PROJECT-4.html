AQL: A Domain-Specific Language for Modeling and Simulating Queuing Networks

  • GitHub Reposotpry: click-here
  • Programinglangauge: C#

Thoughtful planning and optimization of queuing systems is essential to many realworld domains, such as computer networking, manufacturing, customer service or healthcare. Accurate modeling, analysis and simulation of queues is paramount to understand performance bottlenecks, system behavior and resource allocation. Several tools exist to solve these problems, yet they often construct a false dichotomy: Some are general-purpose platforms with steep learning curves, while others are simplistic and do not align with modern queuing theory

This paper introduces AQL, a domain-specific programming language designed for construction and simulation of complex queuing networks with simplicity and expressiveness, without losing mathematical accuracy needed for performance analysis.

Both the theoretical foundations for queuing analysis and existing tools are explored, before detailing the design, definition and implementation of AQL. Lastly, a demonstration is presented showing how AQL can simplify the simulation of an airport queuing system and extract important performance metrics for future planning and optimization.
